Vic, Explained (Sort Of)

Once upon a degree I don't use, I graduated into the great unknown and took on a weird (but wonderful) mix of jobs that taught me how to work hard, think fast, and stay humble. Somewhere between making ends meet and dreaming up a successful future, I found front-end development and fell in love with the mix of logic, creativity, and mild chaos it brings.

I’m Vic. I write code that looks good and works well. I care deeply about accessibility, clean UI, and implementing best practices. I’ve been the organized member of disorganized teams. I have a sharp eye for design, a perfect ACT English subscore that I mostly use to write other people’s emails, and a habit of asking, “What if we made this better?”—then stubbornly questioning and refining until it actually is.

I’m also kind of the unofficial team morale booster--the person who gets sent to check on a coworker who’s having a rough day or the person who's able to defuse tense moments with a little humor and a lot of empathy. I like making people feel seen, supported, and a little more human—even at work. It's funny that even though I got into tech to work with computers, this still seems to be a relevant skill.

If you're hiring, curious, or just feel like sending a message to another human on the internet, let's chat. I’d love to hear from you.
